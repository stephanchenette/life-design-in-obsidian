# Life Design Interview Guide

This guide is for a structured Life Design conversation between a facilitator and an interviewee.

The goal is not to give advice. The goal is to help a person describe current reality clearly enough to make better decisions.

## Facilitator Role

The facilitator is:

- a guide, not a guru
- a mirror, not a consultant
- a keeper of structure and psychological safety

The facilitator should ask clear questions, stay curious, and avoid prescribing solutions too early.

## Recommended Flow

- Introduction: 5 minutes
- Twelve categories: 8 to 12 minutes each
- Closing synthesis: 5 to 10 minutes

This can be done in one long session or split into multiple sessions.

## The Five Core Questions

Ask these in every category.

### 1. Current Reality

On a scale of 1 to 10, how satisfied are you with this area right now?

What does it look like today?

### 2. Premise

What do you believe is fundamentally true in this area?

What assumptions or non-negotiables are driving your behavior here?

### 3. Vision

If this category were working extremely well in three to five years, what would that actually look like?

Describe it concretely.

### 4. Purpose

Why is that important?

What becomes possible elsewhere in life if this category improves?

### 5. Strategy

What are the two or three highest-leverage actions, habits, or decisions that would move this forward?

## The Twelve Categories

Use the following framing prompts if helpful.

### 1. Health and Fitness

This is the physical base layer: sleep, strength, mobility, energy, recovery, and nutrition.

### 2. Intellectual Life

This includes learning, reading, curiosity, knowledge formation, and creative or analytical output.

### 3. Emotional Life

This covers emotional regulation, resilience, triggers, self-awareness, and your day-to-day internal climate.

### 4. Character

This is about integrity, consistency, courage, discipline, and whether your actions align with your values.

### 5. Spiritual Life

This can include religion, meditation, nature, meaning, ritual, or any deeper organizing sense of purpose.

### 6. Love Relationship

This is your primary intimate partnership and the quality of communication, alignment, care, and trust within it.

### 7. Parenting

This is how you guide and shape the next generation, or how you intend to.

### 8. Social Life

This includes friends, peers, community, belonging, and the quality of your social environment.

### 9. Financial Life

This covers money, planning, security, investing, spending behavior, and your relationship to financial freedom.

### 10. Career

This is the work you do in the world, the value you create, and the direction of your professional energy.

### 11. Quality of Life

This includes home, environment, adventure, beauty, fun, rhythm, and the everyday texture of living.

### 12. Life Vision

This is the integrated picture of how the other categories fit together into a coherent life.

## Best Practices

- Let silence do some work.
- Ask for examples when answers stay vague.
- Separate current reality from idealized storytelling.
- Look for cross-category effects.
- Note keystone categories and bottlenecks.

## Closing Questions

At the end of the interview, ask:

- Which category matters most right now?
- Which problem is actually upstream of the others?
- What are the three most important next actions?
- What should be revisited next month?
