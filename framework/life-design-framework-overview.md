# Life Design Framework Overview

This document describes a Life Design framework implemented in Obsidian.

There are many versions of this kind of framework. Some use fewer categories. Some use more. One influence on this implementation is Lifebook by Jon and Missy Butcher, but the broader point is not loyalty to one branded method. The point is to create a durable system for thinking intentionally about the major areas of life and revisiting them over time.

## The Core Idea

Most life-planning systems fail when they become static documents.

The real value does not come from describing an ideal life once. It comes from building a repeatable process for:

- clarifying what matters
- assessing current reality
- identifying bottlenecks
- setting next actions
- revisiting the system on a regular cadence

## The Categories

This implementation uses twelve categories:

1. Health and Fitness
2. Intellectual Life
3. Emotional Life
4. Character
5. Spiritual Life
6. Love Relationship
7. Parenting
8. Social Life
9. Financial Life
10. Career
11. Quality of Life
12. Life Vision

You do not need to use these exact labels. The categories are useful because they force a broader and more integrated view of life than career-only or productivity-only systems.

## The P-V-P-S Structure

Each category is reviewed through four lenses:

### Premise

What do I believe in this area?

This is where assumptions, values, inherited beliefs, and constraints become visible.

### Vision

What does a strong version of this area actually look like?

The more concrete the vision, the more useful it becomes.

### Purpose

Why does this matter?

This is the link between the category and motivation.

### Strategy

What do I need to do?

This is where the category becomes operational.

## The Operating Loop

The framework becomes useful when it is embedded in a recurring loop:

1. Maintain a page for each category.
2. Run a monthly review.
3. Rate each category.
4. Capture notes and next actions.
5. Reassess which categories are upstream bottlenecks.
6. Update the strategy.

## What To Watch For

The system is most useful when you avoid three common mistakes:

- treating all categories as equally important at all times
- writing abstract aspirations without operational next steps
- failing to compare this month against prior months

In practice, one category often acts as a keystone constraint. The point of the system is not balanced prose. It is better diagnosis.
